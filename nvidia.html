About NVIDIA

NVIDIA Corporation is an American multinational technology company incorporated in Delaware and based in Santa Clara, California. It was founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. NVIDIA is best known for its Graphics Processing Units (GPUs), which are widely used in gaming, professional visualization, data centers, and automotive markets.

The company's flagship product line, the GeForce series, is popular among gamers and is known for its high performance and cutting-edge technology. NVIDIA also produces the Quadro series for professional workstations, the Tesla series for data centers, and the Tegra series for mobile devices.

In recent years, NVIDIA has expanded its focus to include artificial intelligence (AI) and deep learning, with its GPUs being used to accelerate AI workloads in various industries. The company's CUDA platform allows developers to leverage the power of NVIDIA GPUs for parallel computing applications.
